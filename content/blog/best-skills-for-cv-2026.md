The best skills to put on a CV are a focused mix of hard skills (measurable abilities like data analysis or project management), soft skills (like communication and teamwork), and now digital and AI tools, all matched to the specific job you want. Listing the right skills helps both the recruiter and the software that scans your CV see that you fit the role. This guide breaks down each type with real examples, then shows you how to match your skills to any job description.

## Hard Skills vs. Soft Skills (The Quick Difference)

Before the lists, here are the two main groups. Knowing the difference helps you balance your CV.

- **Hard skills** are teachable, measurable abilities. You can prove them. Examples: writing in Python, building a budget in Excel, or running paid ads. They are often tied to tools, software, or a specific method.
- **Soft skills** are how you work with people and handle your tasks. They are harder to measure but just as important. Examples: communication, teamwork, and problem-solving.

A strong CV shows both. Hard skills get you noticed for the role. Soft skills show you can actually do the job alongside a team.

## The Best Hard Skills to Put on a CV

Hard skills are specific, so they carry a lot of weight. Pick the ones that fit your field. Common, in-demand hard skills include:

- **Data analysis** (reading numbers and spotting trends)
- **Project management** (planning work and hitting deadlines)
- **Spreadsheet skills** (Excel, Google Sheets, formulas, pivot tables)
- **Coding languages** (Python, JavaScript, SQL, and others)
- **Copywriting and content writing**
- **Search engine optimization, or SEO** (helping pages show up on Google)
- **Digital marketing** (email, paid ads, social media)
- **Graphic design** (tools like Figma, Canva, or Adobe Photoshop)
- **Bookkeeping and financial reporting**
- **Foreign languages** (name the language and your level)
- **Customer relationship management, or CRM, tools** (like Salesforce or HubSpot)

Always name the exact tool or method when you can. "Data analysis" is fine, but "data analysis in Excel and SQL" is stronger and easier to verify.

## The Best Soft Skills to Put on a CV

Soft skills tell an employer what you are like to work with. The trick is to prove them later with examples in your experience section, not just list them. The most valued soft skills include:

- **Communication** (writing and speaking clearly)
- **Teamwork and collaboration**
- **Problem-solving**
- **Adaptability** (staying calm when things change)
- **Time management**
- **Leadership**
- **Attention to detail**
- **Critical thinking** (weighing facts to make good decisions)
- **Emotional intelligence** (reading and managing people well)

Do not just claim these. Anyone can write "great communicator." Back it up in your work history, for example: "Led weekly team meetings and wrote client updates that cut back-and-forth emails."

## Digital and AI Skills for 2026

This is the fastest-growing group. Many roles now expect you to work with modern tools, and AI skills stand out because they show you can work faster and smarter. Useful digital and AI skills include:

- **AI tools** (using assistants like ChatGPT, Copilot, or Claude to draft, research, or code)
- **AI image and content tools** (like Midjourney or Canva AI for design tasks)
- **Data visualization** (turning numbers into clear charts)
- **Automation tools** (connecting apps to save time, using platforms like Zapier)
- **Cloud tools** (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, or collaboration apps like Slack)
- **Basic data privacy and security awareness**

One rule: only list a tool if you can talk about it in an interview. Naming an AI tool you have never opened is a risk. Naming one you use every week is a real advantage.

## Example Skills by Field

Skills are not one-size-fits-all. Here is what a good mix might look like in a few common fields.

- **Marketing:** SEO, content writing, Google Analytics, email marketing, social media, AI writing tools, communication, creativity.
- **Software development:** Python, JavaScript, SQL, version control (Git), testing, problem-solving, teamwork, AI coding assistants.
- **Administration and office roles:** Excel, calendar and email management, data entry, CRM tools, communication, organization, attention to detail.
- **Sales:** CRM software, lead generation, negotiation, product knowledge, communication, relationship building, resilience.
- **Design:** Figma, Adobe Creative Suite, UX basics, prototyping, AI design tools, creativity, collaboration.
- **Customer support:** Help desk software, clear writing, patience, problem-solving, product knowledge, empathy.

Use these as a starting point, then swap in the exact words from the job you want.

## How to Match Your Skills to the Job Description

This is the most important step, and most people skip it. Recruiters and hiring software often look for specific skill words. If the job asks for a skill and your CV uses that exact word, you score higher. Here is a simple way to do it:

1. **Read the job description twice.** Highlight every skill and tool it names.
2. **Make a short list** of those skills that you honestly have.
3. **Use the same words on your CV.** If the ad says "stakeholder management," write "stakeholder management," not "dealing with people."
4. **Put the top matches first** in your skills section so they are easy to spot.
5. **Prove the big ones** in your work experience with a quick example.

This tailoring matters because many companies use software to scan applications for keywords. Do not copy skills you do not have just to match the ad. A skill on your CV is a promise you can back up. If you cannot discuss it, leave it off.

## Where to Put the Skills Section

For most people, a short skills section sits well near the top of the CV, just under your summary. That way, a busy recruiter sees your best-fit skills in seconds. If you have many years of strong experience, you can move it below your work history, since your job titles do the heavy lifting. Either way, keep the list clean and scannable. ## Key Takeaways

- The best skills to put on a CV are a mix of hard skills, soft skills, and modern digital or AI tools, all matched to the target job.
- Hard skills prove specific ability; soft skills show how you work. Include both.
- AI and digital skills are increasingly expected, so list the tools you genuinely use.
- Always pull skill words straight from the job description and use the same wording.
- Keep your list focused (around 8 to 12 skills) and back up your top skills with examples.
