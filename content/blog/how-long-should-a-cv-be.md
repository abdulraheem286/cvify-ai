For most people, a CV should be one to two pages long. One page is best early in your career, and two pages work once you have several years of relevant experience. So when you ask how long should a CV be, the honest answer is: long enough to prove you fit the job, and short enough that a busy recruiter reads all of it.

Length is not the real goal. Clarity is. Below you will find the right length for your stage, what to cut, and how to keep every line earning its place.

## How Long Should a CV Be in 2026?

The safe rule is one to two pages. Most recruiters skim a CV in well under a minute, so a shorter, sharper document usually beats a long one.

Think of your CV as a highlight reel, not a full history. You do not need every job, task, or course you have ever done. You need the parts that match the role you want now.

There are a few exceptions, and we cover those below. But for a standard job application, if you are pushing past two pages, that is usually a sign to trim rather than add.

## What Is the Right CV Length for My Career Stage?

Your ideal length depends on how much relevant experience you have. Here is a simple guide by stage.

| Career stage | Recommended length | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Student or recent graduate | 1 page | Limited work history; keep it focused |
| Early career (1-4 years) | 1-2 pages | A page fills naturally; stretch to two only if needed |
| Mid career (5-15 years) | 2 pages | Room for results without repeating yourself |
| Senior or executive | 2 pages (rarely 3) | Focus on recent, high-impact roles |
| Academic, research, or medical | As long as needed | Publications, grants, and clinical work go in full |

Use this as a starting point, not a strict rule. A tightly written one-page CV can beat a padded two-page one at any stage.

## Why Shorter Is Usually Better

Recruiters and hiring managers read a lot of CVs. A clean, focused document respects their time and makes your best points easy to spot.

Short also helps with the software step. Many companies use an ATS (applicant tracking system), which is software that scans and sorts CVs before a human sees them. A clear, well-structured CV is easier for both the ATS and the recruiter to read. You can learn more in our guide to [an ATS-friendly CV format](/blog/ats-cv-format).

A few reasons brevity wins:

- **Faster to read.** Your strengths are not buried under filler.
- **Looks confident.** You know what matters and cut the rest.
- **Easier to tailor.** A lean CV is quicker to adjust for each job.
- **Fewer weak lines.** Every bullet has to earn its space.

## When Is a Longer CV Okay?

Sometimes more pages are justified. The main exceptions are academic, research, scientific, and medical roles. These often need a full record of publications, grants, conferences, teaching, and clinical experience, so they can run well beyond two pages.

A senior leader with a long, relevant track record can sometimes use a third page too. Even then, keep older roles brief and put the detail into recent, high-impact work.

If you are applying for a normal role in most industries, though, treat two pages as your ceiling.

## What Should You Cut to Fit the Right Length?

If your CV is too long, the fix is rarely a smaller font. It is better editing. Cut anything that does not help you win this specific job.

Start by removing these:

- Jobs from more than 10-15 years ago, unless they are directly relevant.
- Everyday duties that any person in that role would do.
- A long list of hobbies that add nothing to the application.
- Full mailing addresses, references, and "references available on request."
- Repeated skills or phrases that appear in several bullets.

Then tighten what is left. Lead each bullet with a result, use plain words, and keep lines short. ## How Do You Make a Short CV Still Feel Complete?

A short CV should feel focused, not thin. The trick is to fill the space with proof, not padding.

Use strong, specific bullets. Instead of "responsible for social media," write what you did and what happened: "grew Instagram followers and boosted post engagement over six months." Numbers and outcomes make one line do the work of three.

Keep your layout clean and consistent. Clear headings, sensible spacing, and a single column help both people and software read your CV in order. A ready-made layout removes the guesswork here. You can pick from our [templates](/templates) and drop your details straight in.

Finally, tailor each version. Match your CV to the job by mirroring the words in the posting. When the content is relevant, one or two pages is plenty.

## Key Takeaways

- Aim for one to two pages: one page early on, two pages once you have several years of relevant experience.
- Students and recent graduates should target a single page.
- Two pages is the standard sweet spot for mid-career and most senior roles.
- Academic, research, and medical CVs are the exception and can run longer.
- If your CV is too long, cut old and generic content before you shrink the font.
